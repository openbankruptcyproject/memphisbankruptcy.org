Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13: Which Is Right for You?

Chapter 7 -- Fresh Start

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) in about 3-4 months. You must pass a means test based on your income.

  • Discharge in 3-4 months
  • Eliminates credit cards, medical debt
  • Must pass the means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Filing fee: $338
Learn more about Chapter 7 →

Chapter 13 -- Payment Plan

Chapter 13 lets you keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for saving a home from foreclosure or a car from repossession. No means test required.

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep your home and car
  • Catch up on missed payments
  • No means test requirement
  • Filing fee: $313

Memphis Chapter 13: Key Statistics

Memphis has some of the highest Chapter 13 dismissal rates nationally:

  • 79.6% dismissal rate -- nearly 4 out of 5 Chapter 13 cases end without a discharge
  • Only 20.4% of filers receive a discharge -- meaning only 1 in 5 successfully complete their plan
  • 53.5% are prior filers -- more than half have filed at least once before
  • Only 1.4% file pro se -- nearly all filers have attorney representation, yet the dismissal rate remains high
  • An estimated 4,861 cases may involve potential 1328(f) eligibility violations

These numbers indicate that a significant number of Memphis residents file Chapter 13 cases that do not reach completion, often after making payments for months or years.

What to Expect When Filing

The Process at a Glance

  1. Credit counseling -- You must complete a credit counseling course within 180 days before filing. Cost: about $15-25.
  2. File your petition -- Your attorney files the petition, schedules, and statements with the court. The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and foreclosures.
  3. 341 meeting -- About 30 days after filing, you attend a brief meeting of creditors. The trustee asks questions under oath. Most last 5-10 minutes.
  4. Debtor education course -- You must complete a second financial education course before discharge. Cost: about $10-25.
  5. Discharge -- Chapter 7: about 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year plan.

How Much Does Bankruptcy Cost?

The total cost depends on the chapter you file and your specific situation:

  • Chapter 7 filing fee: $338 (can request installment payments)
  • Chapter 13 filing fee: $313 (can be paid through your plan)
  • Attorney fees: Typically $1,000-$1,800 for Chapter 7; $2,500-$4,000 for Chapter 13 in Memphis
  • Credit counseling: $15-25 per course (two required)

Fee waivers are available for Chapter 7 filers whose income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Chapter 13 dismissal rate in Memphis?

The W.D. Tenn. has a 79.6% Chapter 13 dismissal rate -- one of the highest in the nation. Only about 1 in 5 filers successfully complete their plan. Full statistics.

How much does bankruptcy cost in Memphis?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees typically range from $1,000-$1,800 for Chapter 7 and $2,500-$4,000 for Chapter 13. Full cost breakdown.

Will I lose my house if I file bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Tennessee's homestead exemption is $5,000 (individual) or $7,500 (family). This is one of the lowest in the country. If your equity exceeds the exemption, your home could be at risk in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you can keep your home. See all exemptions.

Can I keep my car if I file bankruptcy?

Tennessee's vehicle exemption was increased to $10,000 in 2024. If your car equity is below that limit, you can keep it in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you can keep your car and catch up on payments. Learn about exemptions.

How long does bankruptcy take?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months. Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year plan. The 341 meeting happens about 30 days after filing. Full timeline.
